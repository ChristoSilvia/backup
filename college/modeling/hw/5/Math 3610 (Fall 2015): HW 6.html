HW 6


  1. Please read Chapter 3 of UW Lecture Notes before the Lecture on 10/15/15.

  2. Study the the 3 Matlab files (1,2,3) modeling the chemical concentration in a closed network of interconnected reservoirs.

    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Given the vector of reservoir volumes (V) and the vector of initial concentrations (c0), what will be the asymptotic concentration (c_ultimate) in each reservoir as t --> +infinity ?

    Will the convergence to (c_ultimate) be monotone in each reservoir? 

    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Suppose there is an additional cleaning plant, which pumps the water out of Reservoir # 1, removes the chemical, and pumps the purified water back into Reservoir # 1 again. Suppose this plant processes 0.005 km^3 of water per day. How would this change the matrix A? Modify the 2nd file to reflect this change & rerun the experiment. How long will it take until the concentration in all of the reservoirs falls below 0.2 ? Plot the corresponding solutions.

    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Now return to a "closed system" case (i.e., with no cleaning plants). 
    Modify the 3rd file to use the following parameters:
    V = [1,2,3,4,5,6];
    t_star = 300;
    amplitude=1;

    Find the minimal total mass of the chemical (m0) such that,
    if you correctly distribute it among the reservoirs at the time t=0,
    then at the time t=t_star,
    a) the concentration in Reservoir #6 = 0.4;
    b) the concentration in the other 5 reservoirs falls into the interval [0.3, 0.4].

    [Hint: to complete this problem, you will need to learn/understand the "linear superposition principle" and then employ the function linprog, which you have already used in HW2.]

    Find the correct initial vector of concentrations (c0) and the asymptotic concentration (c_ultimate).  
    Plot the corresponding solution to verify that all concentrations will remain in the interval [0.3, 0.4] for all t > t_star.

    -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    (Extra credit)
    Is the latter property generic? 

    I.e., suppose you are allowed to choose any two numbers a,b in the interval [0.3, 0.4] such that a < b.  Now suppose you require the concentration in Reservoir #1 at the time t=t_star to lie in the interval [a,b].  Leaving the constraints on all other concentrations to be exactly the same as in the previous sub-problem, you can again use linprog to find the corresponding c0. Furthermore, suppose that c_ultimate also lies in the interval [a,b]. 

    Will the Reservoir#1 concentration remain in [a,b] for all t > t_star ?   
    Will this hold for every choice of a & b ?

  3. Return to the cell phone reception modeling problem.  Study the provided Matlab scripts and find the best location for the cell tower to maximize the average reception for the known density of stationary callers.  (Use Matlab's  fminbnd function to do this.

    Extra credit:      my Matlab scripts list several possible extensions; e.g.,
    * optimizing w/ respect to several different density functions simultaneously;
    * optimizing the average caller reception with the constraint on the minimal level of reception level provided to everyone. Modify the code to solve either of them.  (Matlab functions from the optimization toolbox will be useful; here is an overview.)

  4. Suppose that the cell tower location is fixed at (0,0) and a walking caller starts from the location (-2,0) and has to be at the location (0,-2)  T minutes later.   Suppose that this caller's speed of motion cannot exceed V.   What is the best path you can find to maximize the average reception quality during the call?

    [    Note: this problem is intentionally open-ended.  I am not asking you to guarantee the optimality of your proposed path, but please write a script to evaluate its corresponding relative reception quality & explain why you think this path is "good" even if not "the best".]

    This Problem is not due on 10/15/15.  I am listing it already now just to start you thinking about it.  You are welcome to submit your (preliminary? incremental?) solutions at any point before 11/24/15.
